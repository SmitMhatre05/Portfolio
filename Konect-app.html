Overview

Through our initial research, we found that:
  • 75% of high school students felt unprepared for post-secondary education
  • 53% of university students changed their major at least twice
  • 37% were unsure if they were on the right career path
  • 30% were not following any career path at all

According to YouScience, these statistics indicated to us that there is a significant gap in support for students navigating their future careers.

At the core of the issue, we realized that students simply donâ€™t know what they donâ€™t know. They lack the exposure to various career fields, making it difficult for them to make informed decisions.

To tackle this issue, we created KONECT - a platform that helps individuals discover career-related events; such as competitions, seminars, and networking opportunities tailored to their goals and aspirations.

User Categories

We identified three key user groups to base our research on: first year university students, upper-year students, and recent graduates.

Pain Points

Our user research revealed several common pain points. One interviewee, who is an upper year university student, shared that despite platforms like LinkedIn, connecting with the right people in her field was difficult. She noted that finding individuals who are both approachable and relatable is challenging, and suggested that better mentoring tools could improve the social experience.

A first-year University of Waterloo student discussed his struggle to secure an internship for summer 2025, feeling disconnected and intimidated by professionals. He finds platforms like LinkedIn and Waterloo Works inadequate for mentorship and networking, and expressed a desire for a more specialized platform with private messaging. He believes mentors encouraging event attendance could boost his confidence as well.

Another interviewee who graduated from university emphasized the importance of networking, particularly in the tech industry, but highlighted how younger students often struggle with confidence and fear rejection. She advocated for informal events like coffee chats and better access to networking opportunities. She also stressed the need for inclusivity and better mentorship.
