User Research

Through our initial research, we found that 75% of high school students felt unprepared for post-secondary education, 53% of university students changed their major twice, 37% were unsure if they were on the right path, and 30% were not following any career path at all, according to YouScience. These statistics made it clear to my team and I that there is a significant gap in support for students navigating their future careers.

At the core of the issue, we realized that students simply don’t know what they don’t know. They lack the exposure to various career fields, making it difficult for them to make informed decisions. To address this, we created KONECT – an app designed to help high school students explore diverse career pathways by connecting them to events such as networking opportunities, seminars, and competitions.

Pain Points

Our user research revealed several common pain points. One interviewee, who is an upper year university student, shared that despite platforms like LinkedIn, connecting with the right people in her field was difficult. She noted that finding individuals who are both approachable and relatable is challenging, and suggested that better mentoring tools could improve the social experience.

A first-year University of Waterloo student discussed his struggle to secure an internship for spring 2025, feeling disconnected and intimidated by professionals. He finds platforms like LinkedIn and Waterloo Works inadequate for mentorship and networking, and expressed a desire for a more specialized platform with private messaging. He believes mentors encouraging event attendance could boost his confidence.

Another interviewee who graduated from university emphasized the importance of networking, particularly in the tech industry, but highlighted how young professionals often struggle with confidence and fear rejection. She advocated for informal events like coffee chats and better access to networking opportunities through educational institutions. She also stressed the need for inclusivity and better mentorship.
